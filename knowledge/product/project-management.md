# Project Management (PMBOK 7)

[Source: WORK/KNOWLEDGE/PM/CAPM/PMBOKGuideSeventhEd_ENG.pdf]

PMBOK 7th edition (PMI, 2021) shifts from process-based to **principles-based** standard. Focus moves from process compliance to value delivery outcomes across predictive, adaptive, agile, and hybrid development approaches.

## The Standard: 12 Principles of Project Management

1. **Stewardship** — diligent, respectful, and caring management of project resources and stakeholder interests
2. **Team** — create a collaborative project team environment with shared ownership
3. **Stakeholders** — engage effectively; understand and balance stakeholder interests continuously
4. **Value** — focus on the intended value and outcomes, not just deliverables and outputs
5. **Systems thinking** — recognize, evaluate, and respond to system interactions; projects are part of larger systems
6. **Leadership** — demonstrate adaptive leadership behaviors suited to the situation
7. **Tailoring** — tailor development approach, processes, and governance to the project context
8. **Quality** — build quality into processes and deliverables; prevent defects over inspecting them out
9. **Complexity** — navigate complexity through approaches that allow for adaptation and response
10. **Risk** — optimize risk responses; balance threat minimization and opportunity maximization
11. **Adaptability** — embrace adaptability and resiliency; build capacity to respond to change
12. **Change** — enable change to achieve the envisioned future state; manage change fatigue

## PMBOK Guide: 8 Project Performance Domains

| Domain | Core concern |
|---|---|
| **Stakeholders** | Engagement strategies; influence map; satisfaction measurement |
| **Team** | Leadership styles; team development; conflict resolution; distributed teams |
| **Development Approach & Life Cycle** | Predictive vs. adaptive selection criteria; hybrid design; phase gates |
| **Planning** — | Just-enough planning; rolling wave; iterative planning in adaptive environments |
| **Project Work** | Execution; procurement; change; knowledge management |
| **Delivery** | Scope, requirements, quality; value release; MVP in adaptive contexts |
| **Measurement** | Metrics selection; EVM; OKRs; leading vs. lagging indicators; forecasting |
| **Uncertainty** | Risk, ambiguity, volatility; VUCA framing; agility as risk response |

## Development Approach Spectrum

`Predictive` ←————————————————→ `Adaptive`
- **Predictive**: fixed scope, variable time/cost; plan-driven; best when requirements stable
- **Iterative**: repeated cycles refining solution; requirements clarify through iterations
- **Incremental**: deliveries in usable subsets; value sooner; requirements moderately stable
- **Adaptive (Agile)**: frequent delivery; high change tolerance; backlog-driven
- **Hybrid**: combine elements; often agile execution within predictive program/portfolio governance

## Tailoring Framework

Tailoring = selecting, adapting, and applying appropriate processes/practices for the specific project context. Factors: organization type, industry, team size, regulatory environment, geographic distribution, stakeholder risk appetite.

**Tailoring process**: start with organizational standard → select approach → tailor delivery → implement improvements.

## Models, Methods, and Artifacts (MMA)

New section in 7th ed. replacing prescriptive process groups with a toolkit:
- **Models**: situational leadership, communication, motivation, change, complexity
- **Methods**: data gathering, analysis, estimation, meetings
- **Artifacts**: strategies, logs, plans, diagrams, reports

## PMI Talent Triangle (updated 7th ed.)
- **Ways of Working**: technical PM skills, agile, hybrid, design thinking
- **Power Skills**: leadership, stakeholder engagement, communication, collaboration
- **Business Acumen**: commercial awareness, strategic alignment, benefits realization
