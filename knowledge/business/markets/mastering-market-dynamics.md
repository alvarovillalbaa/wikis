**Monopoly thesis:** Market end-state is binary — monopoly or irrelevance. The only valid long-run objective is monopolization. Paths: network effects, switching costs, proprietary data/IP, regulatory capture, scale economics.

**Non-linear customer journeys (BCG):**
- The linear funnel (Awareness → Consideration → Action) force-fits a non-deterministic reality; actual journeys are multi-touchpoint, stage-overlapping, and path-variable.
- Each stage hosts dozens of competing surfaces: search, social media, livestreams, display, word of mouth, packaging, endorsements, product sampling, in-store, relational, email, video, in-app.
- Implication: effective market strategy optimizes touchpoint density and moment-of-need presence, not funnel stage ownership. Being at the right moment in a fragmented journey beats owning a single stage.
