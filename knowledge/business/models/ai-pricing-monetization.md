**AI Pricing & Monetization (Bessemer Venture Partners)**

**Structural constraint:** AI gross margins are 50-60% vs SaaS's 80-90%; every inference query incurs non-trivial cost. Unit economics that don't work at 10 customers won't work at 1,000 — include founder time and support costs from day one.

**Charge metric as strategic statement:**
| Metric | Signal | Cost risk |
|---|---|---|
| Consumption (per token/call) | Technical buyer control | Low |
| Workflow (per completed task) | Discrete value unit | Medium |
| Outcome (per successful result) | Maximum value alignment | High |
Left→right: more cost risk, tighter customer alignment. Direction of travel for market-leading AI products is outcome-based.

**Three AI business model archetypes:**
- *Copilots*: enhance humans → seat or consumption pricing
- *Agents*: execute full workflows autonomously → outcome/workflow pricing; decouples output from headcount
- *AI-enabled Services*: automation + human oversight → hybrid; passes efficiency savings to customers

**Soft ROI trap:** Copilot advisory that doesn't close the loop creates renewal-cliff risk as 2025 pilots reprice in 2026 against undemonstrated value. Hard ROI + measurable outcomes command premium pricing and defensible renewals.

**Seven pricing principles:**
1. Value-delivered beats access-granted — abandon pure seat-based SaaS
2. Hybrid + tiered models win — base subscription for predictability, usage tiers for expansion capture
3. Account for inference costs — real marginal cost per query, not theoretical
4. Reframe from cost reduction to capability unlocking — positions in new AI budget categories alongside IT
5. New success metrics: resolution rate, autonomous completion %, developer acceptance rate (not just ARR/CAC)
6. Charge metric becomes GTM North Star — aligns sales, CS, and product on one outcome
7. Get paid for work completed, not access granted

**Finding the price through friction:** Raise incrementally until prospects say "we have to think about that." Stop one step before it kills deals. This is how multi-billion dollar companies discovered their sweet spot — not spreadsheet modeling.

**Hybrid formula:** `Platform fee (2x delivery cost) + outcome credits` — balances margin floor and expansion upside.

**Pricing complexity trap:** Design one model that scales 10→1,000 customers without custom deals spiraling into operational debt.

**Examples:** Intercom Fin ($0.99/ticket resolved), EvenUp (per completed legal doc), Leena AI (outcome-based after abandoning consumption), Sett.ai (per creative module + share of winning ad spend).
