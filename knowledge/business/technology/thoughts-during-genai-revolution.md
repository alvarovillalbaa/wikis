**GenAI productivity ceiling:** Human throughput is the binding constraint; speed gains are secondary. Second-order productivity comes from vertical learning curves and quality amplification via clean, current data.

**Differentiation moat:** AI-native apps are high-variable systems — every system prompt token shifts final UX. AI wrappers are harder to clone than traditional SaaS codebases. Technology portability (provider independence) is the risk to architect against.

**Network effects + cultural shift:** Both are changing simultaneously; new equilibrium not yet clear.

**Data pipeline view:** Collection/measurement is binary (culture-dependent: measure or don’t). Data preparation/transformation is fully AI-eliminable. Visualization is automatic once preparation exists; serves two endpoints — stakeholder reporting and data-informed decision-making.

**Labor polarization:** 10% of workforce doing 80% of work → widening wealth gap, low poverty risk in abundance era.

**Code entropy:** Legacy reusable code is no longer a moat (new code is cheap to generate). Entropy reduction toward maximally efficient code IS a current moat — LLMs don’t navigate deep dependency trees and over-abstract toward simplest paths.

**Backwards planning:** High-risk hypotheses require clear objectives + backward-chaining from outcome to minimum necessary conditions.

**Market timing:** SaaS at trough of disillusionment; recovery contingent on demonstrated value capture outpacing panic dynamics.
