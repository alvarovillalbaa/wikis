# Board Meetings

Good board meetings convert asymmetric context into strategic judgment; bad ones turn expensive people into a reporting audience.

## Compiled Truth
- Keep the room small and relevant: roughly three to five decision-useful board participants beats broad observer attendance.
- Send the deck three days ahead, expect it to be read, and keep reporting below the meeting's center of gravity.
- KPI discipline matters: fewer than ten core metrics is enough for alignment; the live meeting should spend more than half its time on one or two strategic questions.
- Pre-wire friction: the CEO should call each board member for about 15 minutes before the meeting to surface concerns, avoid reactive debate, and focus the room.
- Open with "good, bad, ugly" plus prior-board carryover: good is progress, bad is known miss with a plan, ugly is unresolved risk with no obvious fix.
- The ugly section is often the highest-value part because cofounder conflict, missed strategic bets, or structural trust issues rarely surface through reporting.
- Closed investor sessions can improve CEO feedback quality when they are short, honest, and converted back into useful guidance.
- Long LatAm-style reporting boards with many voices and delayed conflict compress the real work into too little time.
