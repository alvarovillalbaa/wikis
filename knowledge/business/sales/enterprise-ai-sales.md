# Enterprise AI Sales

Enterprise AI sales is a risk-transfer motion: buyers already believe AI matters, but they distrust pilot theater, unclear ROI, weak data boundaries, and uncontrolled workflow change.

## Compiled Truth
- Maturity wedge: most enterprises sit between pilot sprawl and measured productivity; sell the move from experimentation to governed production, not generic AI adoption.
- Pilot trap: demos fail when they avoid messy data, exceptions, permissions, and user judgment; winning workflows are bounded, repetitive, measurable, and human-reviewable.
- Clous wedge: candidate screening, structured interview evidence, HR policy retrieval, performance-review synthesis, and employee-support escalation are narrow enough to prove.
- Category frame: "HR agent system with controls" is stronger than "HR copilot" because it foregrounds governance, observability, permissions, auditability, and lifecycle control.
- Buyer psychology: champions are buying career-safe outcomes; slideware loses to referenceability, product proof, measurable time saved, and legal or trust defensibility.
- Stakeholder map: sell HR value and IT control in parallel; bringing CIO/security late increases perceived implementation and data-risk debt.
- Value depth: the largest gains come from redesigning work, not accelerating old process steps. Interview feedback becomes evidence capture plus calibration; HR Q&A becomes policy retrieval plus decision trail and escalation.
