Goethe-Zertifikat B2 exam structure, task types, timing, and scoring — from the official Goethe-Institut Modellsatz (7th updated edition, July 2014) and the Cornelsen Prüfungstraining Goethe-Zertifikat B2 practice book.

---

## What the Certificate Proves

- B2 = fourth level of the six-level CEFR scale (A1 → C2); "self-sufficient language use"
- Passing demonstrates ability to use standard German (überregionale Standardsprache) in private, social, academic, and professional contexts
- Can be taken worldwide; assessed according to uniform Goethe-Institut criteria

**B2 competences after passing:**
- Understand complex spoken standard German (telephone, radio) on concrete and abstract topics; extract relevant information
- Read a wide range of texts including short notices/ads and long complex specialist texts, commentary, reports
- Write structured letters clearly on complex topics; correct errors in formal letters written by others
- Give well-structured spoken presentations on general topics and areas of personal interest
- Participate actively in informal discussions, take and defend positions

---

## Exam Overview

| Component | German name | Duration | Points |
|---|---|---|---|
| Reading | Lesen | 80 min | 25 |
| Listening | Hören | 30 min | 25 |
| Writing | Schreiben | 80 min | 25 |
| Speaking | Sprechen | 15/10 min | 25 |

- **Total:** 100 points; passing threshold = 60 points (60%)
- **Critical rule:** minimum 45 points in written components (Lesen + Hören + Schreiben) AND minimum 15 points in Sprechen — both floors must be met independently
- Written exam is group format; Sprechen is either pair (15 min) or individual (10 min)

## Component 1: Lesen (Reading) — 80 min, 25 pts

### Aufgabe 1 — Selektive Informationsentnahme (15 min, 5 pts)
- **Task type:** Zuordnung (matching)
- **Text type:** 8 short texts (Kurztexte) such as articles, notices, ads (labeled A–H)
- **What to do:** Match 5 people (each with a described need/interest) to the most suitable organization/text
- One correct match per person; "negativ" if no suitable text exists; one correct answer only
- **Skill tested:** selective information extraction — scanning for relevant details

**Modellsatz LV1 example (topic: environmental/nature organizations in Germany):**
- 8 organizations: A Info Fluglärm (RDF Frankfurt), B Anti-Atom-Plenum Berlin, C VDG Gewässerschutz, D UMKEHR e.V. (Verkehr), E Wasserschutz EU, F DVL Landschaftspflege, G Leisere Luftkorridore, H Alles für die Vierbeiner (Tierschutz)
- 5 persons: old dogs on farm, wind energy info, bio-farmer marketing, Frankfurt airport noise, lake pollution

### Aufgabe 2 — Entnahme von Hauptaussagen und Einzelheiten (25 min, 5 pts)
- **Task type:** Multiple-Choice (dreigliedrig) — 3 options per question (a/b/c), one correct
- **Text type:** Article, Sachtext (informational/factual text)
- **What to do:** Answer 5 comprehension questions (items 6–10) about the text
- **Skill tested:** understanding main ideas and details

**Modellsatz LV2 example (topic: Die Antarktisstation Neumayer III):**
- Station: 2000 km from South Pole, 200m ice sheet, open 4 months/year (November–March active season up to 50 people; 8-month overwinter season 9–11 researchers)
- Team: meteorologists, geophysicists, atmospheric chemists, engineers (mechanical/electrical/IT), cook, doctor (who is also station leader)
- Selection: expert committee; qualifications and psychological stability matter, not gender or nationality
- Central task: Langzeituntersuchungen (long-term studies) — data for future generations; full conclusions may take 100 years

### Aufgabe 3 — Erkennen von Meinungen oder Standpunkten (25 min, 5 pts)
- **Task type:** Alternativantwort — binary: positiv (a) or negativ/skeptisch (b)
- **Text type:** Stellungnahme (opinion piece), Kommentar, commentary
- **What to do:** Determine how the author evaluates 5 aspects (items 11–15)
- **Skill tested:** recognizing tone, attitude, standpoints from nuanced text

**Modellsatz LV3 example (topic: "Kinder contra Freiheit" — low birth rates in Germany):**
- Author is skeptical of daycare shortage as main cause of low birth rates ("Kaum. Wer sich von Herzen ein Kind wünscht...")
- Evaluates men as caregivers positively (improvement since Goethe's time)
- Skeptical that financial state incentives raise birth rates
- Core argument: in a freedom-oriented performance society, parenthood has become a risky life choice

### Aufgabe 4 — Syntaktisch und semantisch korrekte Textergänzung (15 min, 10 pts)
- **Task type:** Lückentext mit offenen Lücken — supply the missing word (open cloze, NOT multiple choice)
- **Text type:** Bericht (report) or similar
- **What to do:** Reconstruct the text by writing the single missing word for each of 10 gaps (items 16–25) in the margin; only grammatically correct answers are scored; only ONE word per gap
- **Skill tested:** syntactic and semantic text completion; grammar and vocabulary knowledge

**Modellsatz LV4 example (topic: "Salat? Typisch Frau! – Steak? Typisch Mann!"):**
- Text about gendered food choices; examples given: "mit" (item 01), "mag" (item 02); items 16–25 are the actual exam items

## Component 2: Hören (Listening) — 30 min, 25 pts

### Aufgabe 1 — Selektive Informationsentnahme (8 min, 10 pts)
- **Task type:** Raster mit Lücken (grid with gaps — correct/supplement information)
- **Text type:** Gespräch oder Nachricht auf Anrufbeantworter (conversation or answering machine message)
- **What to do:** Listen ONCE; correct wrong information or fill missing information in a pre-printed table (items 1–5); item 0 is a worked example
- **Skill tested:** targeted listening for factual details (dates, times, places, prices)

**Modellsatz HV1 example:** Walking tour schedule table (Wanderprogramm) — 6 entries with date, route, distance, meeting point, registration details, price; blanks at positions 1–5 (e.g., price for Seeburger See, meeting point for Bärenpark, destination name for 2–5 Juni week, meeting point for 31 Juli, price for Nichtmitglieder Sternwanderung)

### Aufgabe 2 — Entnahme von Hauptaussagen und Einzelheiten (22 min, 15 pts)
- **Task type:** Multiple-Choice (dreigliedrig) — 3 options per question (a/b/c)
- **Text type:** Radiosendung (z. T. monologisch) — radio broadcast, partly monologue
- **What to do:** Listen TWICE; answer 10 MC questions (items 6–15); mark answers on sheet, transfer to Antwortbogen at end of Hören section
- **Skill tested:** extracting main ideas and details from authentic radio content

**Modellsatz HV2 example (topic: smoking/tobacco — interview with Dr. Rettke):**
- Questions 6–15 cover: why people start smoking, US tobacco industry accountability, Germany's situation, paradoxical citizen behavior, bar smoking ban motives, Dr. Rettke's awareness work results, social/family influences, methods to discourage youth smoking, recommended measures, tobacco advertising policy

## Component 3: Schreiben (Writing) — 80 min, 25 pts

### Aufgabe 1 — Freies Schreiben nach 4 Leitpunkten (65 min, 15 pts)
- **Task type:** Leserbrief (reader letter) — choose ONE of two offered topics
- **What to do:** Respond to a newspaper or internet article; address 4 Leitpunkte (guided bullet points); report, inform, compare, give advice, express opinions
- Length: approximately 180 words (Cornelsen training: SA1 target ~180 words)
- **Skill tested:** structured written production, argumentation, register

**Modellsatz SA1 example topics:**
- Thema 1: "Risiken eines Lottogewinns" — respond to German newspaper article on whether a large lottery win can disturb psychological equilibrium and how to invest wisely
- Thema 2: "Große Mehrheit für strengere Kindererziehung" — respond to internet article on best child-rearing methods; state and justify a recommendation

### Aufgabe 2 — Briefkorrektur (15 min, 10 pts)
- **Task type:** Korrektur lesen — proofread a formal letter
- **What to do:** Find and correct 10 errors (morphological, syntactic, and semantic) in a pre-printed formal letter
- **Skill tested:** recognition and correction of grammar and vocabulary errors

---

## Component 4: Sprechen (Speaking) — 15 min (pair) or 10 min (individual), 25 pts

### Aufgabe 1 — Monologisches Sprechen (12.5 pts)
- **Task type:** Text und drei Leitpunkte (statement prompt + 3 guided points)
- **Text type:** Statement (position/opinion text)
- **What to do:** Give a structured monologue (3–4 min) on the given topic using the Leitpunkte as structure
- **Skill tested:** production — organized monologue, fluency, argument structure

### Aufgabe 2 — Interaktion/Gespräch (12.5 pts)
- **Task type:** Drei Fotos und drei Leitpunkte
- **What to do:** Discuss pros and cons of a proposal shown in 3 photos using 3 Leitpunkte; negotiate and reach a decision with the examiner or partner
- **Skill tested:** interaction — discussion, argumentation, negotiation, taking positions
