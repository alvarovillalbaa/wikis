# Transporte Aéreo

## Metrics

**Demand:** PKT (pasajero·km transportado) = RPM; TKT = RTM for cargo.
**Supply:** AKO (asiento·km ofrecido) = ASM; TKO = ATM.
**Load factor:** PKT/AKO — efficiency metric for airlines. Currently ~85% (majors).
Air transport carries ~7% of world passenger-km; ~0.1% of cargo tonne-km but >35% by value (high-value, time-sensitive).

| Period | Years | Key events |
|--------|-------|-----------|
| Pionero | 1908–1918 | DELAG 1910 (dirigibles, 20 pax); Wright 1903; first regular domestic airline 1914 (St. Petersburg–Tampa, seaplane) |
| Desarrollo | 1919–1945 | DC-3 generalized medium-haul; WWII → aviation boom; Chicago Convention 1944; ICAO created; Boeing 307 (first pressurized airliner, 1940) |
| Crecimiento | 1946–1951 | Constellation (transatlantic); Vickers Viscount (first turbine a/c, 1950) |
| Reactores | 1952–1969 | Comet 1952 (first jet airliner); B707/DC-8 1958 (transatlantic jets); productivity surge |
| Masas | 1969–1980 | B747 1970 (wide-body, long-range); A300 1974 (wide-body, medium); oil crisis killed SST (Concorde, TU-144) commercially; US deregulation 1978 |
| Madurez | 1980–present | A310/A320/B757/B767 (efficiency); B777/B787/A340/A350 (range); LCCs proliferate; cruise speed stabilized ~Mach 0.85 |

## Regulatory Framework

### International Organisations
**ICAO (OACI):** UN agency, created by Chicago Convention 1947. Sets technical standards (annexes), updates norms. Not commercial.
**IATA:** industry association, founded 1945; coordinates tariffs, commercial practices, safety standards, interline agreements, GDS. Represents airlines vis-à-vis ICAO.

### Chicago Convention (1944)
States have sovereignty over national airspace. Defines **9 Freedoms of the Air**:
1. Overflight (A overflight B without stopping)
2. Technical stop (A lands in B for fuel, no commercial traffic)
3. Carry traffic from A to B (bilateral)
4. Carry traffic from B to A (bilateral)
5. A carries B→C en route through its territory (bilateral combinations)
6. A carries B→C through A's hub (bilateral)
7. A carries B→C without touching A territory
8. Cabotaje: A carries between two points within B
9. Domestic pure: A carries within A

Freedoms 1–2 in Chicago; 3–9 in bilateral agreements. Trend: liberalization replacing bilaterals.

### Key Conventions
- **Warsaw (1929):** passenger liability; still partially in force (152 states).
- **La Haya (1955):** raises Warsaw liability limits.
- **Montreal (1999):** current standard for liability; SDR-based compensation; no limits provable negligence.
- **Chicago (1944):** main framework; ICAO created; annexes updated periodically.

### Liberalization Timeline
- **USA (1978):** Airline Deregulation Act → free routes/fares; CAB dissolved by 1985. Effects: cheaper average fares, fewer majors, hub-and-spoke consolidation, LCC emergence.
- **EU (1988–1997):** three packages → single aviation market; open skies within EU by 1997.
- **Open Skies (EU–US 2007):** transatlantic liberalization.
- **Trend:** ICAO norms replace bilateral protectionism; IATA shifts to coordination role.

## Aircraft Classification

### Commercial a/c families
| Category | Examples | Range | Pax |
|----------|---------|-------|-----|
| Long-haul wide-body | B747, B777, B787, A330, A340, A350, A380 | >8000 km | 250–550 |
| Medium-haul wide-body | A300, A310 | 3000–7000 km | 200–350 |
| Narrow-body | A320 family, B737 family | 2000–6000 km | 100–200 |
| Regional jet | CRJ, ERJ, E-Jet | <3000 km | 50–130 |
| Turboprop regional | ATR 42/72, Dash 8, LET | <1500 km | 30–78 |

Market leaders: Boeing + Airbus dominate jets; Bombardier + ATR dominate turboprops.
Engine manufacturers: GE, P&W, Rolls-Royce. Main consortia: CFM (GE+Snecma), IAE (P&W+RR), GE&PW.
Modern efficient engines: CFM LEAP (A320neo/B737MAX), PW1000G (A320neo, A220).

### Aircraft Economic Metrics
**AKO descent curve:** operational cost per seat-km decreases continuously with new-generation aircraft.
**Historical cost order** (decreasing): DC-3, Stratocruiser, Concorde, Constellation, B707, B747, B777-300, A380.
**Load factor economics:** airlines profitable only above ~break-even load factor (~65–70%); revenue management (yield management) optimizes mix of fares.

## Airline Economics

### Revenue
**Yield:** revenue per PKT; influenced by fare class mix, route competition, seasonality.
**Revenue management:** dynamic pricing maximizing revenue across fare buckets; fundamental to LCC model.
**Code-sharing:** two airlines sharing same flight number; allows hub-to-feeder network extension without operating the routes.

### Costs
**Variable costs:** fuel (~25–30%), labor (~25–30%), ATC/airport fees, maintenance.
**Fixed costs:** leasing/depreciation, overhead.
**LCC model:** point-to-point routes, single aircraft type, fast turnaround, no frills, direct distribution → unit costs 40–50% below legacy.
**Hub-and-spoke:** concentrates traffic at hubs → higher frequencies, better connectivity, but congestion and hub-dependence.

### Fleet Management
**Financial leasing (leasing financiero):** medium-long term; purchase option at end; aircraft on balance sheet.
**Operating leasing (alquiler operativo):** short term; no purchase option; off-balance-sheet. Variants: Dry (aircraft only), Wet (+ crew), ACMI (+ maintenance + insurance), Charter.
Largest lessors: GECAS, AerCap, SMBC, AWAS, CIT (~4000 aircraft combined).

## Airports

**Evolution:** simple terminal → modal interchange hub + economic complex.
**Hub function:** connect many city-pairs via a central hub; bank system for connection timing.
**Airport capacity:** limited by runway slots. Slot = entitlement to use airport infrastructure for specified period. Slot-controlled airports distribute slots seasonally (IATA schedule conferences). Incumbent carriers hold grandfathered slots → barrier to entry.

**Revenue:** aeronautical (landing fees, pax charges) + non-aeronautical (retail, parking, rental).
**Busiest airports (pax):** Atlanta, Dallas, Heathrow, Dubai, Beijing, Tokyo, LAX, Paris CDG.
**Traffic distribution:** >90% in N. Hemisphere corridors (USA, EU, Far East). Center of gravity shifting East (Asia growth).

**Environmental impact:** CO₂ responsible for ~50% of aviation climate effect; NOx (short-term forcing), contrails (ice crystal clouds). Aviation ≈ 3.5% of anthropogenic climate forcing. CORSIA (ICAO 2016): carbon offset scheme for international aviation above 2020 baseline.
