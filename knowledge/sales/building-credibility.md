**Sales first principles (HackerNews, 14-year sales operator):**

Sales = golf: optional complexity. Core skill is walking up and hitting the ball.

**Universal purchase motivators (exhaustive):** time, money, sex, approval/love. Every product either saves or creates one of these. If you cannot map your solution to one, you cannot close.

**Aspirin > vitamins:** Aspirin (urgent pain relief) closes every time. Vitamins (optional improvement) close unpredictably. Build a product for the thing people already buy habitually.

**Relationship as tie-breaker:** Given product parity, buyers purchase from who they like. Consistent, authentic presence compounds into preference and trust — and people create reasons to give you money.

**Sales = people + problems, nothing else.** Technology, chemistry, features, certs, pricing structures: irrelevant differentiators. $100T global economy waits for whoever best identifies and solves real problems.
