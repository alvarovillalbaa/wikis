# Pipeline Discipline

Pipeline quality is active buyer momentum plus disqualification pressure: every open deal needs current pain, authority or access, intent, next step, ROI path, and a reason to stay alive.

## Compiled Truth
- Qualified pipeline means real need, decision authority or stakeholder access, recent movement, explicit next step, and intent inside a concrete buying window.
- Best prospects have top-three pain, existing or reallocatable budget, short decision path, low technical blockage, and visible ROI.
- Champion quality requires budget access, internal network, pain ownership, career upside, and process engagement; missing two signals means find or build another champion.
- Urgency should be down-funnel and concrete: free remainder of month, scarce implementation slots, or quarter-bound pricing only works when the buyer already sees value.
- Discounts need reciprocal value: longer term, case-study rights, expansion commitment, referenceability, or implementation commitment.
- Never let "I'll circle back" end a call. Convert ambiguity into either a scheduled 15-minute next step or an explicit no-for-now.
- Follow-ups need a reason: call-specific resource, objection-mapped product update, relevant market signal, customer story, or direct yes/no question.
- Cadence: day 1-2 send the promised resource, day 4-6 ask if evaluation changed, day 10-14 send one substantive angle, then move to monthly or quarterly reactivation.
- Cold emails should read like compact human text: two or three sentences, personalized signal, clear ask, no bulky signature, no "pick your brain" vagueness.
- Dark deals deserve a breakup email that names missed movement, restates specific value, and asks whether the initiative is still a priority.
