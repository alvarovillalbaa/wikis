# Predictable Revenue

[Source: WORK/BOOKS/Predictable Revenue _ turn your business...pdf]

**Central thesis:** Outbound pipeline can be made systematic, predictable, and scalable by specializing sales roles and replacing "smile and dial" cold calling with targeted, multi-touch prospecting (Cold Calling 2.0). The system is the asset, not individual salespeople.

## Cold Calling 2.0

Old cold calling: phone-first, unresearched, interruption-based. Relied on volume → low conversion → rep burnout.

**Cold Calling 2.0 principles:**
- No cold calls (no phone contact with a prospect before email research/response)
- Focus on ICP (Ideal Customer Profile) — precision targeting, not mass volume
- Systematic multi-touch email sequences before any phone engagement
- Goal of first contact: get a referral *up* to the economic buyer, not a meeting

**The referral email formula:** 3–5 sentences max. Who you are, why you're reaching out to *this person specifically*, and asking who in their organization handles the relevant problem. Disarms the prospect — you're not pitching them, you're asking for navigation help.

## Role Specialization (The Core Innovation)

Split the traditional sales role into three specialized functions:

### Seeds (Inbound/Marketing Qualified Leads)
Generated by marketing, product, word of mouth. Not outbound-generated. Highest conversion; longest nurture cycle. Require inside sales to respond to.

### Nets (Outbound Prospecting — Cold Calling 2.0 SDRs)
**Market Response Reps (MRRs) / Sales Development Reps (SDRs):** outbound-only. Responsible for generating pipeline from target accounts. No closing responsibility. Their output = qualified opportunities passed to Account Executives.

**Key metrics for SDRs:** new opportunities created per month, average contract value of opportunities, conversion rate from opportunity to deal.

### Spears (Targeted Outbound — Enterprise/Named Accounts)
High-touch, highly targeted outbound. Often led by a Business Development Representative (BDR). One BDR per 2–4 AEs. Works enterprise accounts that require multi-stakeholder research and long lead times.

## Account Executive (AE) Focus

AEs close deals — they do not prospect. When AEs prospect, two things fail: (1) prospecting gets neglected, (2) closing gets neglected. Role separation makes both functions more efficient.

**AE responsibilities:** manage qualified opportunities, multi-thread complex deals, build champions, handle legal/procurement, close.

## Hot Coals — The Referral Chain

The goal of initial SDR outreach is not to book a meeting with the first contact. It's to move *up* the organization until you reach the economic buyer. Each referral is a "hot coal" — they're warm because someone internal passed them.

Sequence: SDR contacts mid-level → mid-level refers up → SDR contacts senior → senior either refers to the right person or is the buyer → AE takes qualified opportunity.

## Three Keys to Predictable Revenue

1. **Predictable lead generation** — consistent inbound + outbound system generating qualified opportunities at known conversion rates
2. **Sales Development (SDR) team** — the conversion engine between marketing and sales, generating qualified pipeline for AEs
3. **Consistent sales process** — documented, inspectable, improvable stages that allow forecasting

## The 7 Fatal Mistakes

1. Expecting a VP Sales to create a sales process from scratch without founder involvement
2. Not documenting the (even imperfect) current sales process
3. Hiring too many reps before the sales model is validated
4. Confusing lead volume with pipeline quality
5. Not having a CRM, or not using it
6. Letting AEs do their own prospecting before SDR model is validated
7. Not creating a dedicated Sales Development team

## Scaling the System

**Stage gates:**
- First: founder closes first 5–10 deals manually to understand the process
- Second: document what worked → formalize into a repeatable process
- Third: hire first sales reps to run the documented process (not invent a new one)
- Fourth: once conversion rates are stable → hire SDRs to generate pipeline for AEs
- Fifth: predictable revenue machine → expand headcount proportionally to pipeline math

**Pipeline math:** know (a) lead-to-opportunity conversion, (b) opportunity-to-close conversion, (c) average contract value, (d) sales cycle length → derive number of SDRs and AEs needed to hit revenue target.

## Self-Service vs. Enterprise

Two distinct motions with different economics and process requirements:
- **Self-service/SMB:** low ACV, high volume, product-led, minimal sales touch
- **Mid-market/Enterprise:** high ACV, low volume, relationship-led, multi-threaded sales

Mixing both into one sales team produces a mediocre hybrid — neither the efficiency of PLG nor the relationship depth of enterprise.
