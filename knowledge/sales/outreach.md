**Outreach principles (Alvaro's Brain):**
- Messages: maximally brief, credibility-first, single CTA.
- LinkedIn structure: (a) signal (reason for contact) → (b) qualification CTA invoking retrospection, insight, or curiosity.
- Multi-channel, multi-touch, multiple entry points per account.

**Early-stage prospect acquisition (Stripe / Atlas):**

Warm leads convert higher than cold; exhaust network, beta users, and warm intros before cold prospecting. First 10 paying customers establish recurring-need patterns, referral potential, and investor credibility — the minimum viable traction signal.

**Prospect qualification filters:**
- Early adopters: prior startup purchases or Product Hunt engagement signals openness to unproven vendors
- Internet-aligned: web presence, comfort transacting online
- Autonomous budget authority: "Can you independently decide to buy this?" — nonresponse = not ready, not a rejection
- Intrinsically reachable: professionals who expect sales calls (lawyers, consultants); avoid back-office roles resistant to interruption

**Cold email structure (1 paragraph, 3-4 sentences, 1 CTA):**
1. Prove research: reference specific output (presentation, post, website detail) — signals human, not automation
2. Provide 1-2 immediate insights specific to their business
3. Request minimum viable commitment: 15-min call on a specific day

**Follow-up:** Nonresponse ≠ rejection. 1-2 touches/week until engagement. Manual at early stage; automation scales distraction before it scales pipeline.

**Call-to-close by product tier:**
- Low-touch (<$500/mo): ask for the sale on the call; offer concierge onboarding over free trials (trials enable indefinite deferral); 30-day money-back as preferred alternative
- High-touch/complex: rapport → problem discovery → qualification → demo or proposal call

**Congregation intelligence:** Industry associations, conferences, online communities reveal how customers describe their problems — invaluable for positioning language.
