# Searching a CTO vs Becoming One

Our technical co-founder left on February 2024. I did not panic. In fact, I had extreme confidence in what I had to do:

1. Redefine our business model and product roadmap
2. Find the greatest CTO I could find

Well, I panicked for a couple of hours.

In practice, I found someone who seemed great. We onboarded the new role and after 3 days we realized it was not working — he wasn’t ambitious, energetic nor was any good. 

So I lost like 6 full days of work to this. I had to make a hard decision: should I spend more time searching for someone and take the same risk as before? Or should I get the engineering aspect of our business under my control and move it forward?

Even if I found a great CTO, our company would still have the same problem: they are the only one able to develop and maintain our application.

I could not let that happen. And I had to start moving our product forward or our team morale would plummet. We were not progressing in any direction. 

I had to learn Typescript, React and Django (already knew JS and Python).

I had to learn how to develop an application for businesses. Really different to building small projects — what I used to do 4 years ago.

I had to learn about AWS and Heroku and Vercel.

And now lately I’m learning about data centers, GPUs and others. It’s been an awesome learning journey.

And now I can finally say that we have a product I’m proud of. Maybe because I built it myself. But also because the team has believed in it. Now we execute.
